The Destemmer

This top part is the destemmer. Grapes are poured in and the machine separates the stems from the berries, making sure only the fruit continues on for winemaking.

Crushing After Destemming

After the grapes are destemmed, they are gently crushed to release their juice and begin the fermentation process.

Open Fermentation for Red Wine

The process where red wine is created in open fermentation. During this stage, crushed grapes (must) are left in open-top containers, allowing natural yeasts and oxygen to interact with the juice. The skins, seeds, and sometimes stems remain in contact with the juice, extracting color, tannins, and flavor. The cap (grape skins) is regularly punched down to keep it moist and promote even fermentation. Open fermentation is traditional for red wines and helps develop complex aromas and structure.
